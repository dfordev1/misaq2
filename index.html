Everyone starts here — no background required

What if every promise
you make is already a covenant?

You do not need Arabic, seminary training, or prior theology to begin. The Qurʾān and Sunnah speak constantly about promises, trusts, and solemn bonds (ʿahd, mīthāq, amānah, ʿuqūd). This free site shows that idea in plain language — then lets you explore diagrams, short Qurʾān notes, and a simple quiz.

Explain it to me Skip to 3 steps اردو میں پڑھیے

In one minute

  1. Allah binds humanity by lordship and testimony — the famous “Am I not your Lord?” moment (Q 7:172).
  2. Prophets and revelation spell out how that bond is kept: worship, honesty, justice.
  3. You live it daily — wages, marriage, speech, secrets, contracts. Keep or break.
  4. Judgment Day opens the file: what you promised, what you betrayed.

We call this working picture the Mithaq Paradigm. It is built from Islamic sources first — not Christian “covenant theology” copied into Islam.

Why this matters for ordinary life

If you have ever said “I’ll call you tomorrow,” delayed someone’s pay, held a secret, signed a form without reading it, or swore casually by Allah — you already live inside this field. The diagrams simply name what you already feel: fidelity vs betrayal.
